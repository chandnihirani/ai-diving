## Reflections

The dive was amazing. We did a descent with the rope again, and I practiced the floating buoy: I was basically holding the buoy the entire time, winding and unwinding the string to make sure it was floating, then wound it up as we went upwards towards the ascent. Overall I felt confident. It was easy for me to stay at my 5 m safety stop. In previous dives I usually start floating up at 5 m and struggle to stay at 5 m on my own; this time I was completely able to stay at 5 m by myself with no issues.

## Q&A summary

- **SMB (floating buoy):** First time deploying/managing an SMB; no pool practice beforehand. Felt simple and not complicated. Held the buoy the whole dive, wound/unwound the line as needed and on ascent.
- **Drift:** Little to no current on this dive—mainly an SMB practice dive, so you didn’t get a strong “drift” experience. A proper drift dive in current would be a separate step later.
- **5 m safety stop / buoyancy:** You stayed at 5 m on your own this time. What helped: feeling more relaxed, taking deeper and longer breaths (instead of shorter, quicker ones), and consciously focusing on breathing out to help hold position. You also stopped the habit of constantly adding and dumping air; you left the inflator alone and stayed stable.
- **Recommendations:** On future dives, keep using the same breathing approach (deeper, slower; use the exhale to fine-tune buoyancy) and avoid unnecessary inflator fiddling. When you get a real drift dive with current, treat it as a chance to feel how the SMB helps the boat track you and to practice staying with the group in current.
- **What to look out for:** In actual current, SMB and buoyancy management are harder—one thing at a time. If you do a stronger drift soon, brief with the guide on where to deploy the SMB and how to stay with the group.
