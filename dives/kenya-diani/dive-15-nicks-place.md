## Reflections

**Why this dive:** After the deep dive (required, task-focused, not much to see), stayed on the boat for a second dive while others did theirs. Chose a fun recreational dive—no tests, just enjoyment and practice.

**Buoyancy and camera:** Buoyancy felt really perfect. Instructor had said you tend to forget buoyancy when you're focused on the camera, so made a point of practicing both: getting low for good shots while still maintaining trim and buoyancy. Worked on focus on camera and focus on buoyancy together. Felt excellent—really got the hang of it again.

**Marine life:** Huge shoals of fish clustered together—giant balls of yellow fish, orange fish, grey fish. Two turtles. One animal that looked exactly like a sea snake; pretty sure it was a sea snake, though aware that some fish can mimic other things. Overall fantastic variety.

**Feeling:** Excellent dive. Really proud of myself and excited for what comes next.

---

## Q&A summary

- **Mantra (breathing, arch down, BCD):** Still running it consciously on this dive—not yet fully automatic. Good to keep reinforcing until it becomes second nature.
- **Possible sea snake:** Kept distance; aware they can be dangerous. Also open to the possibility it was a fish that looked like one. Observed without approaching—sensible approach.
- **What to look out for:** Keep using the mantra on the next dives; over time it should require less conscious effort. For unknown or potentially hazardous wildlife, same approach: observe, don’t close in.
