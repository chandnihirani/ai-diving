## Q&A summary

- **Horizontal trim:** Focusing on it made a clear difference—you felt much more stable. You’re getting a little better at it.
- **Left ear (equalizing):** On land the left ear still makes a popping sound when you equalize, as if something is in there; it still clears. You’re hoping it resolves by tomorrow.
- **Recommendations:** Tell your instructor before the deep dive: “Yesterday my left ear was slow to equalize and still feels a bit tight—I can clear on land but there’s a pop.” They can advise whether to dive, delay, or get a quick check. If tomorrow the ear is still tight or painful, get it checked before going deeper. If it feels normal and the instructor is happy, go ahead with a slow descent and equalize early and often.
- **What to look out for:** Any pain, blockage, or worsening before the deep dive = don’t push it. Popping that clears and no pain is often mild and can settle overnight.
