## Reflections

**Purpose:** This was the required Adventure Deep Dive—a test dive to check narcosis levels and ability to think clearly at depth (31 m).

**Tasks at bottom:**
- **Color palette:** Look at the palette and note which colors had disappeared. Mostly red and yellow were gone (as expected at depth).
- **Math test:** About 5 addition, 5 multiplication, and 5 division calculations at the bottom. Did them really well and easily—no problem. Good sign for mental clarity at 31 m.

**Buoyancy:** Felt like a big improvement. Was able to kneel down on the sand and focus on the calculations without going up, going down, or bouncing. Held a stable, neutrally buoyant position the whole time—something that’s been a challenge on earlier dives. Felt like a massive step forward.

**Stingrays:** While doing the math test, two stingrays came by—one massive, one smaller. First thought was “Is that a real stingray?” They felt almost surreal in the moment. Instructor pointed them out too, so they were definitely real—a nice little surprise during the test.

**Overall:** Dive was entirely focused on the deep-dive exercises, so no other exploration, but that’s the point of the deep dive: check narcosis and clear thinking at depth. Overall it was a great dive—tasks went well, buoyancy was solid, and the stingray visit was a bonus.

## Q&A summary

- **Why buoyancy felt so much better:** Breathing—long, consistent inhales and exhales instead of short breaths—and using breath to control buoyancy rather than constantly adding/removing air from the BCD. Trim also helped: intentionally arching the back downwards to stay flat and straight (instead of a hunched “mountain” shape) made it easier to hold position and stop bouncing.
- **Current:** The tough part was the **rope descent**—dragging down in the current felt really difficult, like you could fly off the rope. Once on the bottom, the current was not an issue; you were stable for the tasks.
- **Recommendations / self-talk for future dives:** (1) “Focus on breathing, focus on breathing.” (2) “Don’t touch the BCD unless you absolutely need to.” (3) “Arch down, arch down, arch down.” Use this as a pre-dive or in-water mantra to keep breath control and trim as the default.
- **What to look out for:** On rope descents in current, expect it to be the most physically demanding part; once you’re at depth and neutral, it should ease off. If you notice yourself reaching for the BCD out of habit, pause and try a breath cycle first.
