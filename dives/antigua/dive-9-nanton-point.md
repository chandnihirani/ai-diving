## Reflections

Buoyancy was fine for most of the dive, but towards the end I kept floating up and had to keep releasing air from my BCD. That hadn't happened the day before, even though the sites were roughly in the same area. I also ran out of air a little faster than the day before—possibly because this was a deeper dive (max depth 21 m). This was the first time I've ever been on a dive where, during the safety stop and ascent, I actually had to use oxygen from the dive instructor.

## Q&A summary

**Recommendations**
- **Buddy diving:** All nine dives so far have been with an instructor; you're not yet used to diving with a peer (shared decisions, watching each other). Ask your next instructor or shop when divers typically start with a non-instructor buddy and what to learn or practice first. No need to rush—something to plan for as you progress.
- **Gas:** On guided dives the instructor leads, but stay aware: ask "What's your rule for when we turn back?" and "What pressure do you want me to have at the safety stop?" so you understand turn pressure and having enough for ascent + safety stop. On deeper dives or when the cylinder is low, the tank gets lighter and BCD/suit gas expands on ascent—watch your ascent rate and dump early.
- **Buoyancy:** The fast ascent here was empty cylinder + expanding gas. In Advanced (e.g. Peak Performance Buoyancy), mention you'd like to practice controlling ascent and safety stop with a light tank so the situation feels familiar next time.
