## Reflections

Buoyancy was fine for most of the dive, but towards the end I kept floating up and had to keep releasing air from my BCD. That hadn’t happened the day before, even though the sites were roughly in the same area. I also ran out of air a little faster than the day before—possibly because this was a deeper dive (max depth 21 m). This was the first time I’ve ever been on a dive where, during the safety stop and ascent, I actually had to use oxygen from the dive instructor.

---

## Q&A summary

**Buoyancy and ascent:** Buoyancy was fine for most of the dive. Mid-dive (around max depth) there was a first phase of floatiness—slightly moving up and having to work to stay down—which the instructor addressed by adding 1 kg (5 → 6 kg); after that, fine at depth. The second phase happened on ascent toward the safety stop: despite dumping BCD and not adding air, ascent became too fast and the instructor had to hold the diver at 5 m. Gas had run out by then, so the safety stop was completed sharing the instructor’s gas.

**Interpretation:** The diver attributed the fast ascent to the combination of an empty (lighter) cylinder and ascending (expanding air in BCD/suit), not to a skills issue. It was the first time running out of gas on a dive and they don’t often dive to this depth, so it was processed as a situational first rather than a recurring concern. No particular worry in the moment because the instructor had control.

**Highlight:** First time seeing sharks (blacktips)—very exciting; looking forward to future dives with more variety of sharks.
