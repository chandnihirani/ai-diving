## Bucket list destinations

This list is intentionally expandable. It is not capped at 10 if there are other world-class destinations you want built into the tracker.

### 1. Maldives - Maldives

- Why it is here: classic warm-water pelagic destination with currents, channels, blue water, sharks, mantas, and easy repeat-trip appeal.
- Typical readiness target:
  - **AOW**
  - **Nitrox recommended**
  - **25-30 dives**
  - Comfortable SMB use
  - Comfortable with mild-to-moderate current and blue water
- Status logic:
  - `Ready now` when you meet the dive-count range, have Nitrox or a clear plan to do it there, and current/SMB evidence looks solid.

### 2. Raja Ampat - Indonesia

- Why it is here: flagship biodiversity + current destination and the original basis for the readiness tracker.
- Typical readiness target:
  - **AOW**
  - **Nitrox recommended**
  - **50 dives**
  - **10+** open-water SMB deployments
  - Drift/current comfort consistently **4-5/5**
  - **2-3** reef-hook dives
  - **2-3** negative-entry dives
  - Buoyancy/trim consistently **4-5/5**
- Status logic:
  - `Ready` only when both dive count and current-handling evidence are clearly there.

### 3. Philippines (Moalboal / Malapascua / similar) - Philippines

- Why it is here: high-value warm-water bucket-list option with iconic reefs, sardine runs, thresher-shark trips, and generally more forgiving progression than the hardest liveaboards.
- Typical readiness target:
  - **AOW**
  - **20-25 dives**
  - Comfortable at **25-30 m**
  - Solid buoyancy/trim
  - Basic SMB confidence
- Status logic:
  - This is one of the first long-haul bucket-list items you may become ready for.

### 4. Fiji (Rainbow Reef / Somosomo Strait) - Fiji

- Why it is here: soft corals, colour, current, and classic South Pacific walls.
- Typical readiness target:
  - **AOW**
  - **25-30 dives**
  - Comfortable drift diving
  - Good buoyancy in current
  - SMB confidence
- Status logic:
  - `Ready` once you are clearly comfortable in current, not just capable of completing the dive.

### 5. Komodo - Indonesia

- Why it is here: one of the best warm-water progression-to-advanced destinations and a natural return after your first Indonesia trip.
- Typical readiness target:
  - **AOW**
  - **30-40 dives**
  - Repeated drift/current experience
  - Strong SMB habits
  - Good situational awareness in changing current
- Status logic:
  - Easier Komodo diving may come sooner; full Komodo confidence needs stronger current evidence.

### 6. Sipadan - Malaysia

- Why it is here: iconic walls, schooling fish, turtles, and warm-water prestige without being as current-heavy as the toughest liveaboards.
- Typical readiness target:
  - **AOW**
  - **25-30 dives**
  - Good buoyancy near walls
  - Comfortable mid-depth profiles
  - Basic current comfort
- Status logic:
  - One of the more realistic mid-term bucket-list targets.

### 7. Red Sea Liveaboard (Brothers / Daedalus / Elphinstone style) - Egypt

- Why it is here: classic shark/liveaboard trip with blue water, current, negative entries, and repetitive diving.
- Typical readiness target:
  - **AOW**
  - **Nitrox strongly preferred**
  - **30-40 dives**
  - Negative-entry familiarity
  - SMB confidence
  - Blue-water comfort
- Status logic:
  - `Ready` requires stronger boat routine and entry/exit confidence than a normal resort trip.

### 8. Palau - Palau

- Why it is here: warm-water current destination with hooks, channels, sharks, and drift-heavy diving.
- Typical readiness target:
  - **AOW**
  - **Nitrox recommended**
  - **35-50 dives**
  - Reef-hook familiarity
  - Negative-entry familiarity
  - Strong drift confidence
- Status logic:
  - Similar readiness family to Raja Ampat, but slightly less demanding at the threshold level.

### 9. Socorro - Mexico

- Why it is here: giant pelagics, blue water, liveaboard routine, and exposed open-ocean diving.
- Typical readiness target:
  - **AOW**
  - **Nitrox strongly preferred**
  - **50 dives**
  - Blue-water comfort
  - Calm boat handling and repetitive diving stamina
  - Strong SMB and situational awareness
- Status logic:
  - A later-stage bucket-list target, even if depth itself is not the hardest part.

### 10. Galapagos - Ecuador

- Why it is here: one of the biggest long-term goals in diving, but also one of the clearest "earn it" trips.
- Typical readiness target:
  - **AOW minimum; Rescue desirable**
  - **Nitrox strongly preferred**
  - **75+ dives**
  - Strong current/drift comfort
  - Negative entry confidence
  - Blue-water confidence
  - Cold surge / tougher-condition composure
- Status logic:
  - Treat this as a long-term target, not a near-term one.

### 11. Great Barrier Reef - Australia

- Why it is here: one of the world's iconic reef systems with huge variety, from easier coral reef diving to more advanced outer-reef and liveaboard trips.
- Typical readiness target:
  - **AOW** preferred
  - **20-25 dives** for stronger outer-reef or liveaboard value
  - Solid buoyancy/trim around coral
  - Comfortable boat diving
- Status logic:
  - This can range from `Ready now` for easier resort-style reef diving to `Close / one trip away` for the more rewarding outer-reef version.

### 12. Cocos Island - Costa Rica

- Why it is here: one of the elite big-animal destinations in the world, known for hammerheads, exposed conditions, current, blue water, and demanding liveaboard diving.
- Typical readiness target:
  - **AOW minimum; Rescue desirable**
  - **Nitrox strongly preferred**
  - **75+ dives**
  - Strong current/drift comfort
  - Blue-water confidence
  - Negative-entry confidence
  - Calm liveaboard routine and situational awareness
- Status logic:
  - Treat this in the same longer-term readiness tier as the hardest shark- and current-heavy expeditions.

### 13. French Polynesia (Fakarava / Rangiroa) - French Polynesia

- Why it is here: iconic South Pacific shark passes, blue water, reef passes, and one of the most famous pelagic-heavy tropical destinations in the world.
- Typical readiness target:
  - **AOW**
  - **Nitrox recommended**
  - **35-50 dives**
  - Comfortable drift / pass diving
  - Blue-water confidence
  - Good SMB habits
- Status logic:
  - Readiness depends heavily on whether you are doing easier lagoon/reef diving or the more serious pass-diving version.

### 14. Tubbataha Reefs - Philippines

- Why it is here: one of the world's standout reef systems, known for pristine walls, sharks, schooling fish, and liveaboard-only access.
- Typical readiness target:
  - **AOW**
  - **Nitrox strongly preferred**
  - **40-50 dives**
  - Comfortable liveaboard routine
  - Good current handling
  - Solid buoyancy/trim
- Status logic:
  - A more demanding bucket-list Philippines trip than the easier Moalboal / Malapascua path.

### 15. Cabo Pulmo / Baja liveaboard - Mexico

- Why it is here: top-tier North American bucket-list diving for schooling fish, sea lions, pelagics, and a strong gateway into bigger-animal expedition diving.
- Typical readiness target:
  - **AOW**
  - **25-35 dives**
  - Good boat comfort
  - Blue-water comfort helpful
  - Basic current confidence
- Status logic:
  - Often achievable earlier than Socorro while still feeling like a major bucket-list step.

### 16. Jardines de la Reina - Cuba

- Why it is here: one of the Caribbean's signature shark and reef destinations, with remote liveaboard diving and excellent marine life density.
- Typical readiness target:
  - **AOW**
  - **Nitrox recommended**
  - **30-40 dives**
  - Comfortable boat/liveaboard routine
  - Good buoyancy around reefs
  - Basic-to-moderate current confidence
- Status logic:
  - A strong mid-tier expedition destination before the hardest Pacific current trips.

### 17. Malpelo - Colombia

- Why it is here: one of the hardest-core big-animal bucket-list destinations on earth, famous for sharks, blue water, current, and exposed ocean conditions.
- Typical readiness target:
  - **AOW minimum; Rescue desirable**
  - **Nitrox strongly preferred**
  - **75+ dives**
  - Strong current/drift comfort
  - Blue-water confidence
  - Calm liveaboard routine and situational awareness
- Status logic:
  - Treat this alongside Cocos and Galapagos as an advanced expedition-level goal.

## How future readiness checks should work

When you ask, "How close am I to being ready for the bucket-list items?", answer destination by destination using this format:

- `Ready now`
- `Close / one trip away`
- `Needs more current practice`
- `Needs Nitrox`
- `Longer-term goal`

Examples:

- "Ready for the Philippines now."
- "Close for Maldives; Nitrox plus a few more current dives would make it cleaner."
- "Raja Ampat still needs more drift repetition, more SMB reps, and reef-hook / negative-entry evidence."
- "Galapagos is still a longer-term build."
- "Komodo has been sampled, but not yet completed in the full bucket-list sense."
